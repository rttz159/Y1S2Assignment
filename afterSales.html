Major Service

Your vehicle probably needs a little more attention after years or mileage traveled on the road. A major service is usually undertaken every 2 years or 40,000km, whichever comes first, replacing parts that are meant to wear, such as spark plugs, fuel filters, drive belts, transmission oil, power steering fluid, clutch fluid, radiator coolant, and engine mountings.

Air Conditioning

Something as simple as changing the air conditioning filter in your car can prolong its efficiency as well as improve the air quality in a car. Air conditioner circulates and filters air, removing pollutants and mold from the air in-cabin. Maintain the air conditioner regularly will minimize the irritants that trigger allergies or asthma or respiratory diseases.

Tyres

Tyre tread pattern, depth and pressure are equally important to maintain good grip and right pressure on the road. Simple maintenance can help to keep drivers and other road users safe.

Lubrication

Changing engine oil and it’s filtered regularly is one of the easiest and most effective way to prolong the life of an engine.It reduces friction, prevents oxidation, clean, cools and seals the gaps between components in an engine. Over time, the engine oil loses its lubricating performance due to heat and contamination by metal powder and soot and it will only get worse if you ignore or delay your engine oil change.

Brakes

Properly functioning brake systems are crucial. Brakes must be routinely inspected and consistently maintained so they operate and perform in good, safe and proper working order. Improperly installed or poorly maintained brake systems can reduce braking efficiency, posing serious risk to public safety on our roadways.
